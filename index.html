About the Artist

Painting Stories That
Cannot Be Forgotten

Zakari is a Ghanaian painter whose practice is rooted in the tradition of hand-painted portraiture. Working primarily in oil and acrylic on large-format canvas, he creates images that centre everyday people within compositions of richness and ceremony.

His work draws on the visual language of West African textile, Adinkra symbolism, and classical European portraiture — fusing these into a singular voice that insists on dignity, presence, and the weight of being seen.

Behind the Canvas

The Process

Every painting begins with a conversation. From first sketch to final brushstroke, each work is entirely made by hand.

01

Sketch

Initial drawings capture proportion, gesture, and composition before paint ever touches canvas.

02

Underpainting

A tonal foundation built in raw umber sets the light and shadow before colour is introduced.

03

Colour Layers

Colour is applied in transparent glazes, building depth and luminosity across multiple sessions.

04

Finished Work

Final details — highlights, textures, and pattern work — are resolved by hand before varnishing.
